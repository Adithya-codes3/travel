Top 10 places to visit in this Month:

1.Kedarnath Temple:

Kedarnath Temple, located in the state of Uttarakhand, India, holds great religious significance. It is believed to have been built in the 8th century by Adi Shankaracharya, a revered Hindu philosopher. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas (divine representations) of the deity. It stands at an elevation of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) amidst the majestic Himalayan range. The best time to visit the temple is during the summer months of May to June and September to October when the weather is pleasant. The exact location of Kedarnath Temple is in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, near the Mandakini River

2.Puri Jagannath Temple:

Puri Jagannath Temple, situated in Puri, Odisha, India, is a renowned Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Jagannath (an incarnation of Lord Vishnu). Its history dates back to the 12th century when it was built by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva. The temple is an important pilgrimage site and attracts devotees from all over the world. The best time to visit the temple is during the famous Rath Yatra festival, held in the months of June or July, when the deities are taken out on a grand chariot procession. The temple is located in the heart of Puri, on the eastern coast of India, near the beach.

3.Rama Sethu:

Rama Setu, also known as Adam's Bridge, is a legendary bridge mentioned in the Hindu epic, Ramayana. According to the story, Lord Rama, along with an army of monkeys, constructed the bridge to reach Lanka and rescue his wife, Sita, from the demon king, Ravana. The bridge is believed to have been built with floating rocks and coral reefs. While the existence of the bridge is a matter of debate, it holds significant cultural and religious importance for Hindus. There is no specific temple associated with Rama Setu, but the best time to visit the region is during the winter months of November to February. The bridge is located in the Palk Strait, between the southeastern coast of Tamil Nadu, India, and the northwestern coast of Sri Lanka.

4.Tajmahal:

Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage site, was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century as a mausoleum for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It is an architectural masterpiece, blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles. Constructed with white marble, the monument is adorned with intricate carvings and beautiful gardens. The best time to visit the Taj Mahal is during the cooler months from October to March, avoiding the scorching summer heat. Located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India, the Taj Mahal stands on the banks of the Yamuna River and is a symbol of eternal love and grandeur.

5.Madikeri:

Madikeri, a picturesque hill town in Karnataka, India, has a rich history. It was originally known as Muddu Raja Keri, named after the Haleri king Mudduraja who ruled the region. In the 17th century, it became the capital of the Kodagu Kingdom. The town witnessed the reign of various dynasties, including the Haleri, Lingayat, and British. Today, Madikeri showcases a blend of colonial and traditional architecture, with attractions like the Madikeri Fort and Raja's Seat. The best time to visit Madikeri is during the winter months from October to February when the weather is pleasant and the lush green surroundings offer a captivating experience.
